1. Pick one anchor frame size

Every gallery wall that looks intentional has one frame bigger than the rest that everything else builds around.

2. Match the frame finish, vary the art

Consistency in the frames is what reads as "designed" even when the art itself is a mix of styles.

3. Lay it out on the floor first

Tape the wall dimensions out on the floor and arrange the frames there before a single nail goes in.

4. Finish with one 3D piece

A small shelf or mirror breaks up an all-flat wall and stops it from feeling like a poster board.
